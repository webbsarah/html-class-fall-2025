Let's start with the basics!

Roast

Dark

Dark roast has the richest flavor, with the least acidity.

Medium

Medium Roasts have a balanced mix of the original characteristics of the bean as well as the roasted flavor.

Light

Light roasts have an acidic flavor with lots of fruity, nutty, or floral notes depending on the flavor of the original bean.

Sweetness Scale

Very Sweet

Additives like sugar, milk, syrup, or even honey can add flavor and sweeten the taste of the coffee.

Very Bitter

Neglecting to add sweeteners to your coffee will give a raw, very bitter flavor that some tend to prefer. This will let the original flavor profile of the bean shine through, so make sure to pick a good roast first!

Heat Preference

Hot

Hot coffee, utilizing steamed or frothed milk, will provide a cozy drink to warm the soul. It might enhance the creaminess of the drink, but don't burn your tongue!

Iced

Pouring your coffee into a cup full of ice and using cold milk (or milk alternative) will grant you a refreshing, cold drink that lets the sweetness shine through!
